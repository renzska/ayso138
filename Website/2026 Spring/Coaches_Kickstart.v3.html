Kickstart (Playground and Schoolyard) – Program Guide

Kickstart is AYSO's introductory program for children ages 3–6. Sessions run as a Jamboree and focus on Soccer, Fundamental Motor Skills (FMS), and Social-Emotional Learning (SEL) through movement and play. There are no standings, no scores, and no pressure. Just kids moving, laughing, and falling in love with the game of soccer. This page is your go-to reference for the season. New to Kickstart? Start at the top and work your way down. Coming back mid-season? Jump straight to your session plan for the week.

Session Structure & Timing

Each session is 1 hour maximum, including organizing time and breaks. Here is the standard breakdown:

Segment Duration Notes
Activation & Group Forming ~12 min Activation game as children arrive and introduction to the session activities by Program Leaders
FMS Activity 15 min ~12 min active + 3 min breaks
Game-like Activity 15 min ~12 min active + 3 min breaks
Big Match 18 min ~4 quarters x 3 min active with 3 x 2 min breaks
✅ Adjust play time and breaks as needed based on weather, energy levels, and how your group is doing. Less is more with this age group!

Session Plans

Each session follows the same three-activity format. The SEL theme changes each week. Click any View in Manual button to open that page in the full flipbook.

Session 1 (Week 1)

SEL Theme: Social Awareness  •  Recognize other people's feelings

FMS Activity 1

🕸️ Web-Slinger (Running)

Setup: 15 x 15 yd. grid, scrimmage vests (webs), ball per player for progressions.

  • One player is the Web-Slinger; all others are villains.
  • Web-Slinger chases and throws a vest to "web" a villain.
  • Webbed villains freeze with hands up and legs open until a teammate crawls through their legs.
  • Web-Slinger wins by freezing all villains. Swap often.

Progressions > Players dribble a ball; Web-Slinger must web the ball. Add more Web-Slingers. Add a time challenge.

FMS (Running): Head up, high knees, arms moving, body leaning slightly forward.

Who likes being the web-slinger? Why? Who doesn't? Why?

📄 Detailed Activity Instructions (Page 45)

Game-like Activity 2

⚽ Any Goal (Score!)

Setup: 20 x 15 yd. grid with a goal on each end line.

  • Leader rolls numerous balls onto the field (at least one per player).
  • Players collect a ball and score in any goal.
  • Parents behind goals redistribute balls to keep the game flowing.
  • Goal: players understand the aim of soccer is to score!

Progressions > Reset and score in a different goal. Add a time limit and keep score. Add corner goals.

Activity 3

🏆 The Big Match

Setup: 2v2 or 3v3 game to goal with 4 goals (2 per team).

  • "New Ball" restarts after a goal or ball goes out — opponents 3 yards away.
  • Willing parents help manage balls to keep the game flowing.
  • No corners. Don't enforce strict rules; keep it simple and moving.

Session 2 (Week 2)

SEL Theme: Self-Awareness  •  Identify and manage emotions and behaviors (happy, sad, excited, fun, etc.)

FMS Activity 1

🗣️ Coach Says (Running, Dodging, Twisting/Pivoting)

Setup: 15 x 15 yd. grid, ball per player.

  • Players start on the end-line with a ball at their feet.
  • Leader calls commands — TAKE ONE STEP FORWARD, BALANCE ON RIGHT/LEFT LEG, SIT ON BALL, ONE FOOT ON BALL, SPEED UP, SLOW DOWN, JUMP OVER BALL, etc.
  • Players only react if the command is preceded by "Coach Says."
  • Balancing on each leg is the main focus.

Progressions > Change the command phrase from "Coach Says" to something else.

📄 Detailed Activity Instructions (Page 46)

Game-like Activity 2

🦈 Sharks and Minnows (Dribbling)

Setup: 15 x 15 yd. grid, ball per player for progressions.

  • Minnows have a pinnie tail in the back of their shorts.
  • The shark (Leader) catches minnows by pulling out their tails.
  • When a minnow loses its tail it must leave the grid (the pond).
  • To re-enter, minnows perform a FMS task: balance on one leg 3 sec, bounce the ball 3 times, etc.
  • Start without a ball, then introduce one.

Progressions > Caught minnows become baby sharks.

Did anyone enjoy this game? Why? Did anyone not enjoy it? Why?

Session 3 (Week 3)

SEL Theme: Interpersonal Skills  •  Engage in non-verbal communication with each other

FMS Activity 1

🐸 Frog Jumping (Jumping, Landing, Running)

Setup: Two rows of lily pads (hoops or markers), one per child per row.

  • All children line up in two lines on the start line, facing you at the end line.
  • Kids jump like a frog from lily pad to lily pad in their row, then run around the marker and back to start.
  • Sing a "frog" song while they jump. Children should "croak" while leaping.

Progressions > Jump in different directions: forwards, backwards, sideways. Increase or decrease distance between pads.

📄 Detailed Activity Instructions (Page 52)

Game-like Activity 2

🏦 Bank Robbery (Directions)

Setup: 15 x 15 yd. grid, all balls piled in the center.

  • All players stand by a corner cone.
  • Leader yells "Bank Robbery!" — players sprint to center, collect a ball with their feet, and return it to their corner cone.
  • They return to the center to get another ball. Player who collects the most wins.

Progressions > Players carry balls back, or bounce or roll them back.

Once players get back to their sides, they find a teammate and give them a thumbs up.

Session 4 (Week 4)

SEL Theme: Social Awareness  •  Show that we can help each other

FMS Activity 1

🏅 Treasure Hunt (Skipping, Hopping)

Setup: 15 x 15 yd. grid, orange cones scattered throughout.

  • Start players at one corner cone.
  • Players "skip" around the grid (prioritize technique) collecting as many cones (gold coins) as possible.
  • Once all cones are picked up, player with the most cones wins.

Progressions > Add blue (or other color) cones — players must "hop" over these special cones before picking them up. Worth 100 points each!

📄 Detailed Activity Instructions (Page 50)

Game-like Activity 2

💥 Bumper Ball (Teamwork)

Setup: Standard grid, parents on all four sides as "bumpers."

  • Two teams. Parents stand on the touchlines and goal lines to stop the ball going out of play.
  • Ball stays in play — no corners, goal kicks, or throw-ins.
  • Keep score. Give teams colorful, fun names!

How did we help our teammates in this game?

Session 5 (Week 5)

SEL Theme: Interpersonal Skills  •  Finds ways that help to self-calm (count to 5, ask to sit down)

FMS Activity 1

🍦 Ice Cream Cones (Running, Balance)

Setup: Players split into two sides. Cones and balls in the middle.

  • On Leader command, players run to the middle, pick up a cone and ball to make an "ice cream cone" (parents help as needed), and carry it back to their starting cone.
  • Play continues until all balls are collected. Count how many each player collects.

Progressions > Vary distance. Dribble the ball back. Balance on one leg for 3 sec on the way back. Throw the scoop in the air and catch it!

Why is it important to stay calm when you're getting too excited? You can drop the ice cream out of the cone!

Game-like Activity 2

🐍 Slippery Snake (Dribbling)

Setup: Open area, ball per player.

  • All players form a snake-like line. Each player dribbles their ball to follow the lead player (head of the snake).
  • When the leader yells "Shed Your Skin!" the player at the end dribbles forward and becomes the new front of the snake.

Progressions > Add FMS commands: Stop, balance on left leg, Go!

Session 6 (Week 6)

SEL Theme: Decision Making  •  Understanding we all control our own choices (taking turns, fixing mistakes)

FMS Activity 1

🏹 Hunters and Hunted (Running, Twisting, Pivoting, Dodging)

Setup: 15 x 15 yd. grid, pinnies as tails, one cone (flag/tree) outside the grid.

  • Players tuck tails into the back of their shorts and line up on opposite sidelines as hunters and hunted.
  • On Leader command, hunters chase the hunted and grab tails. Hunted use twists, pivots, and dodging to escape.
  • If caught, the hunted runs outside the grid, around the flag (tree), back into the grid (woods) to retrieve their tail and rejoin.
  • Play 1 min, break 30 sec. Repeat for a few rounds, then switch hunters and hunted.

Progressions > Players dribble a ball.

How did you choose who to chase?

📄 Detailed Activity Instructions (Page 47)

Game-like Activity 2

🐻 Big Bad Bear (Dribbling)

Setup: 15 x 15 yd. grid, two "caves" (marked areas), ball per player for progressions.

  • The Leader is the Big Bad Bear (always growling) and tries to capture players as they dribble around the area.
  • Players are safe inside a cave — but can only stay 10 seconds, then must move to the other cave.
  • Captured players become "Wee Bad Bears" and help catch remaining players. Last free player wins!

Progressions > Start without balls. Only 1 player per cave at a time. All Bears must hold hands while trying to capture remaining players.

Equipment List Per Group

Player Equipment

  • Athletic footwear and shin guards (covered by socks)
  • Shirt turned to the correct color for their group

Field Equipment (Provided by AYSO Board)

  • Cones
  • 4-5 pinnies
  • Four pop-up goals
  • Fun props: hula hoops, beanbags, etc.

Leader Bag "Must Haves"

  • Charged cell phone (in case of emergency)
  • Session plan for the day (digital or printed)
  • Basic first-aid kit (be familiar with the contents)
  • Water (remind parents and players to bring their own)

Arrival Activities

Rather than having kids sit around while everyone arrives, get them moving right away. Arrival activities start on pages 34-43 of the manual.

Examples include:

  • Red Light / Green Light
  • Freeze Tag
  • Hungry Hippos

Start one of these as soon as the first few kids arrive. It sets the tone and burns off early energy before the structured session begins.

Field Tips & Coaching Flexibility

Not every activity will land the same way with every group. You know your kids. You have the ability to adjust on the fly.

⏰ Kids Need a Break?

  • Give it to them whenever they need it.
  • If one child needs a break, send them to their parent and invite them back when ready.

🚫 Activity Not Working?

  • Switch to a different one. Do not push through an activity that has lost the group.
  • Backup Activities:
    Page 44-55 (FMS)
    Page 56-69 (Additional Soccer) Page 70-78 (Game-Like)

🤔 Bored After 5 Minutes?

  • Introduce a progression from the Variations section of the activity.
  • Change the rule, add a ball, add a challenge, or switch to an arrival activity.

📸 Remember: For most of these kids, this is their very first introduction to soccer. Our job is to help them love it. Keep the energy positive, keep the game moving, and celebrate every moment of engagement.

Roles & Communication

Role Responsibility

Assistant Coach / Activity Leader Support the Program Leader as needed. Follow their lead.
Team Manager Send Thursday reminders, manage treat schedule, handle parent communication.

The Coach Administrator will open with a brief introduction to parents at the start of the first session of the first Saturday. After that, Program Leader, the program is yours.
