🤝 Coaches + Referees = Partners in Player Development

Our referees, youth and adult, are volunteers who dedicate their time to make games safe, fair, and fun. Trained referees make for better games, and our players and parents notice the difference. While referees sometimes make mistakes (just like players and coaches do), their rulings on the field are final. Please model the AYSO spirit by treating them with respect and encouragement, never criticism, especially our youth referees, who are still learning and developing just like the players. Positive support helps us recruit, train, and retain referees, which keeps our program thriving. A simple "thank you" or a kind word before and after a match go a long way in showing appreciation and reinforcing the kind of environment we want for every child.

📋 Division Reference Guide

Our Division Reference Guide is a great resource for coaches in all divisions. It covers field sizes, ball sizes, player counts, game formats, and other division-specific details you'll want to keep handy throughout the season.

Note: The guide lists standard player counts, but we have a couple of exceptions for this spring. For 10U, the standard format is 7v7, but if both teams have 11 or more players available at kickoff, that match will be played 8v8. For 12U, the standard format is 9v9, but if both teams have 14 or more players available at kickoff, that match will be played 10v10.

🥅 6U: Team Bags on the Field

6U coaches, your team bag should already be out on the field on game day. If not, you can find them in or near the pod in the southeast corner of the east parking lot. After your game, please make sure to pack everything back up and leave the 6U coaches bag on the east side of the field or by the pod (preferred). Thank you for helping us keep things organized!

🥅 6U and 8U: Fold-A-Goal and Flag Setup and Takedown

We're asking that coaches, assistant coaches, and team managers help set up the fold-a-goals and flags for their game. Here's how it works by field (map attached):

8U Northwest Fields (Northwest A, B, C, and D)

  • Please have the corner flags and fold-a-goals set up before the 9am game — we'd love for the red team (home / first listed) to take the lead on this.
  • After the 9am game wraps up at 10am, we'd ask the blue team (away / second listed) to take everything down and place it on the west side of the bigger field, or by the pod (preferred) in the east parking lot.

8U Southeast Fields (Southeast E and F)

  • Please have goals and flags set up before the 9am game.
  • Everything should be taken down at 11am, after the 10am game wraps up, and placed on the east side of the field or by the pod (preferred).
